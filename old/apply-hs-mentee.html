High School Mentorship Program: Mentee Recruitment

Women in Science and Engineering's (WISE) mission is to support and empower women to achieve their full potential as engineers, scientists, and leaders. We strive to provide an enriched experience for women in the fields of science and engineering through networking events, resume workshops, and design challenges.

WISE provides exciting opportunities with the aim to cultivate a safe environment for growth in both the academic and professional fields. We hold events throughout the year that are open to students of all genders to enhance their networking abilities and give them opportunities to determine what it is within STEM that most interests them.

We ultimately hope to encourage those who are interested in STEM to pursue it with the opportunity to grow in their academic career, and aim to increase accessibility to a field that has historically been male-dominated.

The High School Mentorship program connects high school students across the GTA with a university student at UofT, who they will meet with throughout the academic year to talk about the mentee's goals and the mentor provides advice on how to pursue them.

Applications are due: November 3, 2019

As a mentee, you will...

  • Be paired up with a university student mentor based on your interests, experiences, and your desired field(s) of work or study
  • Participate in resume, application and professional development workshops, design challenges, and social events
  • Build a relationship with your mentor and have a contact to ask any questions if you need guidance
